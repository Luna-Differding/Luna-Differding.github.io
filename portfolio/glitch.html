Glitch Art- Chronology

Statement: Chronology is a work which conveys the origins of humanity and it's developments over time. The work functions as a series or the final compilation of images. Through gradual addition of color, Chronology suggests that we slowly developed the characteristics that make us human. We were once nothing. We then existed simply and animalistically--our purpose to survive and reproduce. Finally, eons later, we are able to exist in a reality were we can indulge ourselves and see the world in technicolor.

Processes

Piece One: â€‹I made this first piece by finding an image of a supernovae which I felt could work as representing the Big Bang, and how I imagine the Big Bang having looked. From there, I was able to take the data from the image and turn it into a sound bit using audacity. To do this I had to open the image in Photoshop, and change it to raw data. This is called data moshing. I put the "echo" effect on the sound bit to distort it, and transferred the data back into an image by bringing the raw data back into photoshop.

Piece Two: The second image I found by looking up images of early humans. I liked this image because the quality of the image was pretty low resolution and seemed crudely put together, which tied in with the idea of humans starting out as simple, unrefined creatures. I glitched this one by taking the data and putting it into my source code editor, where I moved around certain pieces of the code and deleted some pieces altogether in order to distort the image. I then brought it into photoshop where I was able to lay it over the first piece.

Piece Three: The third image was found by looking up silicon valley. I chose this image because it contrasts the second image through it's more organic forms, symbolizing progress from simple, rudimentary shapes to complex, elegant and visually pleasing shapes. Silicon valley is the epitome of human technological advancements, so it represents not only how far we've come but how far we're going. I used data moshing for this one as well using audacity and it added much more vibrancy to the image.
