Phenomenal Intentionality--A Disembodied Experience

Statement

My final project, "Phenomal Intentionality--A Disembodied Experience", serves as an antithesis to Duane H.Davis' piece featured in "50 Concepts for Critical Phenomenology", in which Davis suggests in order to unify our society on issues such as racism and sexism we must focus on eliminating our biases. He asks us to do so by using something called the epoche, in which we imagine ourselves in the third person in order to view ourselves more impartially as we go about our day, making it easier to identify and eliminate our biases. My proposition is that this is an ineffective way to coquer these issues, as it asks us to live inauthentically. By using webcams, I ask the viewer to reflect both literally and figuratively. Utilizing webcams coupled with instructions and statements I want the viewer to consider, my hope is that the viewer realizes this approach asks us to become so wrapped up in ourselves that it is not only selfish, but causes us to be less present and less authentic, thereby hurting our loved ones and ourselves.

Process

I coded this website using HTML, and embedded cameras coded in Java Script. One of the filters has a blurring effect, which symbolizes the good aspects of the viewer, a regular, filterless webcam, which makes the viewers flaws visible, representing the entirety of their character, both good and bad, and lastsly a colorful webcam, which utilizes a shader. This one is symbolic for finding beauty in chaos and accepting the world as is. To check out the project for yourself, click the button below.
